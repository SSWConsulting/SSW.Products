When you need to reduce what a client owes on an invoice (for example adjustments, returns, or goodwill), TimePro supports several approaches.

## Option A: Add a Credit Note

Use this option when you need to formally reduce what a client owes, either against a specific invoice or at the client level for later allocation. Credit notes are the only available method for invoices in locked periods.

You can create a credit note in two ways:

* **From an invoice**\
  Use the "Add Credit Note" button when you want to apply a credit directly against that invoice.

![](/TimePro/add-credit-note-invoice.png)

**Figure: Invoice - Use “add credit note”**

* **From a client**\
  Create a credit note outside of a specific invoice when you want the credit recorded at the client level first, then allocated later if required.

![](/TimePro/add-credit-note-client.png)

**Figure: Client - Use “add credit note”**

## Option B: Add a Discount

Use this option when you want to apply a discount to a new invoice. The purpose is to adjust the invoice value using a separate line item, keeping the original invoice lines intact.

On an invoice, when you click “Add Invoice Item”, you can then choose between:

* **Discount (%)
  **Use a discount (%) to reduce the line item price by a percentage. Enter the discount directly on the product line - TimePro will calculate the reduced amount automatically.

![](/TimePro/add-discount.png)

**Figure: Use a Discount**

* **Negative quantity **or **sell amount**\
  Add a product line and enter a negative quantity (Qty) or sell amount (Sell). The extended amount reflects **Quantity × Unit Sell**, so a negative quantity reduces the invoice balance. Use this when you want the adjustment to appear as a separate line on the invoice.

![](/TimePro/add-invoice-item.png)**Figure: Use a negative quantity or sell amount**
