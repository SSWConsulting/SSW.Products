When you need to reduce what a client owes on an invoice (for example adjustments, returns, or goodwill), TimePro supports several approaches.

## Option A: Add Credit Note

You can create a credit note in two ways:

* **From an invoice**\
  Use the "Add Credit Note" button when you want to apply a credit directly against that invoice.

![](/TimePro/add-credit-note-invoice.png)

**Figure: Invoice - Use “add credit note”**

* **From a client**\
  Create a credit note outside of a specific invoice when you want the credit recorded at the client level first, then allocated later if required.

![](/TimePro/add-credit-note-client.png)

**Figure: Client - Use “add credit note”**

## Option B: Add Invoice Item

The purpose of this option is to separate the original invoice lines from the credit lines.

On an invoice, when you click “Add Invoice Item”, you can then choose between:

* **Use a negative quantity (Qty)**\
  Add a product line and enter a **negative quantity**. The extended amount reflects quantity × unit sell, so a negative quantity reduces the invoice balance. Use this when you are reversing or reducing units of a charge while keeping a positive unit **Sell** price.

![](/TimePro/add-invoice-item.png)

**Figure: Click “Add Invoice Item” and add a negative quantity**

* **Use a negative sell amount (Sell)**\
  Add a product line and enter a **negative sell** (unit sell amount). Use this when quantity stays positive but you need a negative line amount i.e. a fixed dollar credit expressed per unit.

## Choosing an approach

* Prefer **Add credit note (Option A)** when dealing with **past (locked) invoices**, or when your organisation’s process or reporting requires credit lines to be created through the credit-note workflow.
* Use **Add Invoice Item (Option B)** for **active invoices**, where changes need to update the invoice directly. This method immediately adjusts the invoice total using negative quantity or negative sell values.
