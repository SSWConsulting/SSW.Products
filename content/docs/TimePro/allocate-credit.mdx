When you need to reduce what a client owes on an invoice (for example adjustments, returns, or goodwill), TimePro supports several approaches. The **Add invoice item** dialog allows **Qty** and **Sell** to be negative so line amounts can reduce the invoice total.

## Options

1. **Credit note**  
   Use the credit-note workflow (credit product category) when you want a dedicated credit line that is handled as a credit note in the product catalogue. This is the clearest option when the business process expects an explicit credit document or credit-type line.

![](/TimePro/add-credit-note.png)

Figure: Credit-note line added using the credit product category

2. **Invoice item with negative Qty**  
   Add a product line and enter a **negative quantity**. The extended amount reflects quantity × unit sell, so a negative quantity reduces the invoice balance. Use this when you are reversing or reducing units of a charge while keeping a positive unit **Sell** price.

![](/TimePro/add-invoice-item.png)

Figure: Add invoice item — negative **Qty** reduces the line amount when **Sell** stays positive

3. **Invoice item with negative Sell**  
   Add a product line and enter a **negative sell** (unit sell amount). Use this when quantity stays positive but you need a negative line amount—for example a fixed dollar credit expressed per unit.

## Choosing an approach

- Prefer **credit note** when your organisation’s process or reporting expects credit lines to be created through the credit-note path.
- Use **negative Qty** or **negative Sell** when you are adjusting an invoice with a manual line and the maths is clearer one way or the other (units vs dollars per unit). Both are valid; pick the representation that matches how you explain the adjustment to the client.
